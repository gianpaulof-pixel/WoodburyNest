The "$18,000 Question": Do You Qualify for Down Payment Assistance in Woodbury?

Gian "G" Visciglio, REALTOR®
April 18, 2026  ·  Keller Williams Premier Realty  ·  MN License #41031450

If you've been scrolling through Zillow or driving through Woodbury neighborhoods, you've likely done the math. Even with a steady income, saving up a 3.5% or 5% down payment while paying today's rents is a steep mountain to climb.

But here is a perspective shift: Down payment assistance (DPA) isn't just for "low-income" buyers. In Minnesota, many middle-class professionals and even repeat buyers are eligible for significant financial boosts; sometimes upwards of $18,000; that may never have to be paid back if you stay in the home long enough. Repayment terms vary by program, and your lender will explain the specific forgiveness structure that applies to you.

The Eligibility Checklist: 4 Factors for 2026

To provide a true picture of how these programs work, eligibility is generally determined by four specific factors:

Factor 1
Your Financial Footprint

Most Minnesota programs require a minimum credit score of 640. While you don't need a 20% down payment, you are typically required to contribute at least $1,000 of your own funds toward the transaction to show "skin in the game."

Factor 2
Household Income

This is where most people are surprised. In our 11-county metro area, income limits are generous. Many programs allow household incomes up to $152,000 or more depending on household size and program type. If you're a teacher, nurse, or early-career professional in Woodbury, you are likely within the qualifying range. Your lender will confirm the exact limits that apply to your situation.

Factor 3
Home Price Limits

The home you're eyeing must fall under certain price caps, which currently sit at $659,550 for the Twin Cities metro. This covers a vast majority of the townhomes and starter-to-mid-tier single-family homes in our local market.

Factor 4
The "First-Time" Variable

While "Start Up" loans are for those who haven't owned a home in three years, the "Step Up" program is specifically for repeat buyers. There is even a First-Generation bonus through MHFA for those whose parents did not own a home, offering up to $35,000 in some cases. Your lender will identify which program fits your situation.

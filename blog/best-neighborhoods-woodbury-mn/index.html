The Best Neighborhoods in Woodbury, MN

Six Woodbury communities ranked by price point, lifestyle fit, and what the data actually shows. Whether you are upsizing, downsizing, or buying for the first time, this guide tells you which neighborhood actually matches how you live.

Gian "G" Visciglio, REALTOR®
June 19, 2026  ·  Keller Williams Premier Realty  ·  MN License #41031450

Woodbury is one of the Twin Cities' most consistently sought-after suburbs, and for good reason. It ranks at or near the top of Washington County for school performance, public amenities, and long-term resale stability. The South Washington County School District (ISD 833) holds a Niche grade of A-minus and ranks #25 statewide — placing it in the top 10% of all Minnesota school districts. But "Woodbury" is not one neighborhood — it is a collection of distinct communities, each with its own character, price point, and buyer profile.

The six neighborhoods below represent a cross-section of what Woodbury offers: established communities with mature trees and larger lots, newer luxury enclaves with private amenities, and everything in between.

Bailey's Arbor

$300K – $700K+
Single-family, townhomes, twin homes ISD 833 · Liberty Ridge Elementary Pools · Tennis · Nature Trails

Bailey's Arbor is one of Woodbury's most thoughtfully designed neighborhoods. The community was built around its natural surroundings: wetlands, nature reserves, and mature tree lines are woven into the layout rather than cleared for development. Homes reflect that philosophy, with large windows, open floor plans, and outdoor living spaces that actually connect to the landscape.

Price points span a wide range, which makes Bailey's Arbor one of the few Woodbury neighborhoods where a first-time buyer and a move-up buyer can both find something that fits. Townhomes and twin homes start in the low-to-mid $300,000s; single-family homes with larger lots push well past $700,000 depending on finishes and square footage.

Community amenities include multiple pools, tennis courts, parks, and an extensive trail network that connects to the broader Woodbury trail system. The neighborhood's elementary school, Liberty Ridge, holds a GreatSchools rating of 9 out of 10 — among the highest in Washington County. East Ridge High School rounds out the secondary pipeline, also rated A-minus by Niche. Woodbury Lakes shopping and dining is a short drive away, covering everything from Trader Joe's to casual restaurants.

Best fit for: Buyers who prioritize outdoor access and nature-forward design without sacrificing neighborhood amenities. The wide price range also makes it a strong option for buyers who want to get into Woodbury at a lower entry point and still benefit from the school district.

Wedgewood Park / Prestwick

$550K – $1M+
Single-family, custom builds ISD 833 · East Ridge HS Golf Course · Large Lots · Privacy

Wedgewood is one of Woodbury's most established and recognizable luxury communities. What sets it apart from other high-end neighborhoods in the city is its relationship with Prestwick Golf Course: many homes back directly to the fairways, giving residents a scenic backdrop that is genuinely difficult to replicate. It is a well-maintained, mature neighborhood with the kind of landscaping that takes decades to develop.

Architectural styles lean colonial, traditional, and contemporary — often on large private lots that provide separation between homes. Prices range from the high $500,000s into the $1M+ range, with custom-built properties at the higher end. Inventory here tends to be limited, and well-priced homes move quickly when they do appear.

Residents are served by ISD 833 — the South Washington County School District — which carries a Niche A-minus grade and ranks #25 in Minnesota. Secondary students feed into East Ridge High School. Woodbury Lakes is close by for retail and dining. The neighborhood itself is quiet and private — the kind of place where people tend to stay for a long time.

Best fit for: Move-up buyers or established professionals seeking privacy, prestige, and long-term equity stability. The golf course setting is a genuine differentiator, not a marketing term — for buyers who value that backdrop, there is nothing quite like it in Woodbury.

Powers Lake

$500K – $1M+
Single-family, lakefront South Washington Co. Schools Lake Access · Kayaking · Fishing

Powers Lake occupies a category of its own in Woodbury's real estate market. True lakeside living within the city limits is rare, and this neighborhood delivers it without requiring a trade-off on urban convenience. Homes range from traditional to contemporary, many with lakefront views and large private lots. Prices typically run from the $500,000s to well over $1 million for direct waterfront properties.

The lake itself supports kayaking, fishing, and non-motorized recreation. Walking trails and open green spaces extend the outdoor experience beyond the waterfront. Despite the serene, almost resort-like setting, the neighborhood sits within easy reach of Woodbury's shopping and dining districts.

One data point worth noting for buyers considering lakefront properties: waterfront homes in the Twin Cities metro have historically shown stronger long-term appreciation than comparable inland properties. Lakeside inventory is inherently constrained, which provides a degree of supply-side insulation that most suburban neighborhoods do not have.

Best fit for: Buyers who want a distinctive lifestyle anchor — not just a home in a nice suburb, but a specific way of living. Powers Lake is also worth considering as a long-term hold: limited waterfront inventory and persistent buyer demand make it one of Woodbury's most defensible price points.

Highcroft

$500K – $1M
Single-family, newer construction South Washington Co. Schools Modern Finishes · Trails · Parks

Highcroft is Woodbury's answer to buyers who want newer construction at the luxury level without going into full custom-build territory. Homes here are characterized by open floor plans, modern kitchen finishes, and expansive outdoor living spaces. Architecture blends traditional proportions with contemporary detailing — the result is homes that feel current without being trendy.

Pricing typically falls between $500,000 and $1 million, with the range reflecting differences in lot size, square footage, and finish level. The neighborhood is well-positioned relative to Woodbury's dining and retail corridor: Crave, Lunds and Byerlys, and several other high-quality options are close by.

Walking and biking trails connect Highcroft to larger Woodbury park systems, and the neighborhood's layout supports an active daily lifestyle without requiring a car for every trip. For buyers comparing Highcroft to older Woodbury neighborhoods, the primary tradeoff is lot size versus condition: homes here are newer and require less maintenance, but lots tend to be smaller than what you would find in established communities like Wedgewood or Evergreen.

Best fit for: Buyers who prioritize move-in-ready condition and modern finishes over lot size. Also a strong fit for dual-income households that want a polished home without the overhead of a renovation project.

Evergreen

Mid $400Ks – Upper $600Ks
Single-family, 1980s–1990s builds ISD 833 · Woodbury HS Mature Trees · Larger Lots · Quiet Streets

Evergreen is one of Woodbury's most beloved established neighborhoods, and it is often underrated in comparison to the city's newer master-planned communities. Homes were built primarily in the 1980s and 1990s on larger lots, meaning the mature trees and landscaping that newer neighborhoods are still waiting for are already here. The result is a neighborhood character that feels genuinely settled — tree-lined streets, privacy between properties, and a pace that reflects decades of community life.

Home prices run from the mid-$400,000s to the upper $600,000s. Many properties have been significantly updated over the years, offering buyers newer interiors with the lot size and neighborhood maturity that new construction cannot replicate. Two-story layouts with updated kitchens and bathrooms are common.

Families in Evergreen attend Royal Oaks Elementary and Woodbury High School. Tamarack Tap Room and Trader Joe's are nearby, as are Ojibway Park and an established trail network. For buyers who have been looking at new construction and wondering what they are giving up, a walk through Evergreen is instructive.

Best fit for: Buyers who value neighborhood maturity, lot size, and character over new finishes. Evergreen tends to attract buyers who have lived in newer construction before and know what they value most — and for many, that turns out to be the things that time builds, not developers.

Twenty-One Oaks

$600K – $1M+
Custom and semi-custom single-family South Washington Co. Schools Private Clubhouse · Pool · Fitness Center

Twenty-One Oaks is Woodbury's most exclusive newer neighborhood, built with a deliberate emphasis on privacy and luxury. Homes are primarily custom or semi-custom builds, with prices starting in the $600,000s and extending well past $1 million for the largest and most finished properties. Gourmet kitchens, spa bathrooms, and professional-grade outdoor living areas are standard expectations at this price point, not upgrades.

The neighborhood's defining feature is its private amenity set: a members-only clubhouse with a pool, fitness center, and social spaces that function more like a private club than a typical HOA amenity package. Walking and biking trails complete the outdoor offering. Unlike some luxury communities where amenities are on paper but underused, Twenty-One Oaks has a genuinely active social fabric.

Families are served by some of the strongest schools in the South Washington County district. Woodbury Lakes provides high-end and casual dining and retail within a short drive. Inventory in Twenty-One Oaks is consistently limited — when homes come available, they tend to attract motivated buyers quickly.

Best fit for: Luxury buyers who want the privacy and finish level of a custom home alongside the social infrastructure of a private community. If you are comparing Twenty-One Oaks to building custom outside of a neighborhood, the tradeoff is autonomy versus amenity access and built-in community.

How to Choose the Right Woodbury Neighborhood

A few questions worth answering before you narrow your search:

  • How do you actually spend your weekends? If outdoor recreation is central to your lifestyle, Bailey's Arbor and Powers Lake should be at the top of your list. If you want a private clubhouse and social infrastructure built in, Twenty-One Oaks changes the calculus.
  • What is your maintenance tolerance? Newer construction in Highcroft or Twenty-One Oaks requires less short-term upkeep. Evergreen homes may need more, but they often deliver lot size and neighborhood character that newer builds cannot replicate.
  • Which school boundaries matter to you? Most of these neighborhoods fall under the South Washington County School District, but school boundaries for elementary schools differ. This is worth verifying for any specific address before you make an offer.
  • Are you thinking about resale? Waterfront and golf-adjacent properties in Woodbury have historically held value well due to constrained supply. Established neighborhoods like Evergreen also tend to be more resilient in softer markets than newer developments at the urban fringe.

A Note on Schools in Woodbury

The majority of Woodbury falls within ISD 833, the South Washington County School District. It ranks #25 statewide on Niche with an A-minus grade, placing it in the top 10% of Minnesota school districts. All six neighborhoods in this guide are served by ISD 833.

Within the district, elementary school assignments vary by address — you cannot assume that two homes a few blocks apart share the same elementary school. A few reference points from current GreatSchools data:

  • Liberty Ridge Elementary: GreatSchools rating 9 out of 10. Serves Bailey's Arbor and surrounding east Woodbury communities.
  • Royal Oaks Elementary: Serves Evergreen and nearby central Woodbury neighborhoods.
  • East Ridge High School: Niche A-minus. Serves most of east and south Woodbury including Bailey's Arbor and Wedgewood.
  • Woodbury Senior High School: Niche A-minus. Serves central and west Woodbury including Evergreen.

One additional note: a portion of northeast Woodbury falls within ISD 834, the Stillwater Area Public Schools district (also Niche A-minus). Always verify the exact school assignment for any specific address — district boundary lines do not always follow obvious geographic logic.

Sources: Niche.com 2026, GreatSchools.org 2025-26. Ratings are a reference point, not a definitive assessment. Visit schools directly and verify boundaries through ISD 833 before making a purchase decision based on school assignment.

Common Questions

What is the most affordable neighborhood in Woodbury?

Of the six neighborhoods in this guide, Bailey's Arbor has the widest range and the lowest entry point, with townhomes and twin homes starting in the low-to-mid $300,000s. Evergreen is the most accessible for detached single-family homes, typically starting in the mid-$400,000s. Both are served by ISD 833.

Which Woodbury neighborhood has the best schools?

All six neighborhoods in this guide are served by ISD 833, one of the top 10% of school districts in Minnesota by Niche rankings. The specific elementary school varies by address. Liberty Ridge Elementary, which serves Bailey's Arbor, carries one of the highest GreatSchools ratings in the district at 9 out of 10. The district as a whole is strong — the differences between individual schools are smaller than most buyers expect.

How competitive is the Woodbury market in 2026?

Well-priced homes in Woodbury continue to move quickly. A significant share of homes sell within 14 days of listing, and multiple-offer situations remain common in the $400K–$600K range. Luxury properties in Wedgewood and Twenty-One Oaks move more slowly but tend to attract serious, well-qualified buyers when inventory does appear. Contact me for a current NorthstarMLS snapshot for your target price range.

Are there Woodbury neighborhoods without HOA fees?

Yes. Evergreen and portions of Wedgewood and Powers Lake have minimal or no HOA requirements. Bailey's Arbor and Twenty-One Oaks carry HOA fees that cover community amenities — pools, clubhouses, trail maintenance. Whether that tradeoff makes sense depends on how much you would actually use those amenities.

How do I verify which school district a specific home falls in?

The most reliable method is to check directly with ISD 833 using the specific property address — district boundary maps are available through the district's website but can be counterintuitive near the edges. Do not rely on listing descriptions or neighborhood names alone. Elementary school boundaries within ISD 833 do not always align with the broader neighborhood boundaries.
